Mangú

Mangú is a traditional Dominican dish made from boiled and mashed green plantains. It’s usually mixed with a little butter or oil and seasoned lightly with salt. Mangú is often served with toppings like sautéed red onions, fried cheese, eggs, or salami. It’s simple, filling, and a popular breakfast in the Dominican Republic.

Pollo Guisado

Pollo Guisado is a simple and flavorful Dominican stewed chicken dish. It’s made by cooking chicken with garlic, onions, bell peppers, tomatoes, cilantro, and spices like oregano, salt, and pepper. The chicken is browned first, then simmered with tomato sauce and a little water until it becomes tender and the sauce thickens. It’s usually served with rice, beans, or plantains, and is a staple in many Dominican homes.

Tostones

Tostones are fried green plantains that are crispy and salty. They are sliced, fried, flattened, and fried again. People in the Dominican Republic eat them as a snack or as a side dish with many meals.
